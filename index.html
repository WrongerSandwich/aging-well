Current takeaways

The signal, so far.

Only actions supported by completed research sessions appear here.

T1Substances

01

Don’t smoke.
If you do, quit.

The highest-leverage action identified in the project so far.

~3×all-cause mortality
for lifelong smokers

Lifelong smoking costs roughly 10–13 years. Quitting before 40 averts about 90% of excess mortality, with benefit detectable around three years after cessation.

Large prospective cohorts · hard endpoints · high confidence
T1Exercise

02

Get off zero.

Reach about 150 minutes of moderate activity each week. Most benefit is bought by moving from sedentary to active.

19–21%lower all-cause mortality
vs. sedentary

Returns continue toward 300 minutes per week, but diminish. Weekend-warrior scheduling performs similarly when total weekly volume is met.

Prospective cohorts · hard endpoints · high confidence
T1Exercise

03

Train strength
about 2× / week.

A small dose independently lowers mortality. Include high-intensity loading if building bone is the goal.

~13%lower all-cause mortality
at 90–119 min/week

Benefits appear to plateau beyond roughly 120 minutes per week. Moderate loads may maintain bone; high-intensity resistance and impact training is needed to build it.

Cohorts for mortality · RCT surrogate evidence for bone
T1Exercise

04

Train balance
on purpose.

Walking, stretching, or lifting alone do not reproduce the fall-prevention benefit of dedicated balance work.

24%lower rate of falls
with balance training

Balance and functional training reduces falls causally in RCT meta-analysis. Exercise overall reduces fractures by about 25–33%.

Cochrane RCT meta-analysis · high-grade evidence
T1Substances

05

Alcohol has no health-justified floor.

Zero minimizes total harm. If drinking, ≤1/day is a harm-reduction ceiling—not a target.

T2Substances

06

Vaping is harm reduction, not harmless.

Do not start as a non-smoker. For smokers, a complete switch is defensible; dual use buys no measured benefit.

How to read this

Evidence is not
a level playing field.

Every claim is tagged. Mechanism and hype can inform questions, but they do not enter the action ranking.

T1Act on it

Large cohorts, RCTs, and meta-analyses with hard human outcomes.

T2Credible

Smaller trials, consistent observational data, or surrogate outcomes.

T3Inform only

Mechanistic, animal, small, or conflicting human evidence.

T4Do not rank

Plausible but speculative; often adjacent to commercial longevity hype.
