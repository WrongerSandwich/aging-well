# Lever: Nutrition & Metabolic

> Status: RESEARCHED — session 4 (2026-06-19). FIVE hybrid runs (two resumed through usage limits): patterns → harms(meat/sugar/sodium) → beneficial(fiber/fish/coffee) → metabolic(CR/fasting/adiposity/protein) → adiposity/protein/anchors. ~100 claims confirmed.
> Lever complete for ranking. **Defining feature: huge observational associations, tiny causal footprint** — three distinct failure modes (self-selection confounding, food-vs-supplement gap, rodent over-extrapolation) all pointing to the same pragmatic core: avoid the clearly-bad, don't chase superfoods/fasting/supplements. The one large *causally-clean* signal is adiposity (once reverse causation is stripped out).

## Scope
Diet pattern, protein, visceral fat, glucose, lipids, blood pressure. The hub connecting cardio/neuro/renal decline — and the lever most plagued by healthy-user confounding and nutrition-science hype.

- In: dietary PATTERNS (Mediterranean, DASH, plant-forward, Western/ultra-processed); specific FOODS & macronutrients (red & processed meat, added sugar/SSBs, sodium, fiber, fish/omega-3, whole grains); PROTEIN adequacy & the protein/mTOR/IGF-1 longevity debate; COFFEE/caffeine (from substances); CALORIC restriction / intermittent fasting / TRE; body WEIGHT / visceral adiposity / BMI vs mortality; GLYCEMIC / metabolic-syndrome / lipids / BP as modifiable endpoints.
- Out (and where it lives instead): protein/calories to fuel resistance training → `exercise`; ALCOHOL → `substances` (coffee comes here); longevity-hype supplements (NMN, resveratrol, metformin) → T3/T4 holding pen in `synthesis`; PHARMACOLOGIC weight loss (GLP-1) & lipid/BP drugs → `medical-screening`; sleep→appetite → `sleep`; dental erosion from sugar → `oral-sensory`.

## Dose / threshold
**The 80/20 for nutrition is "don't eat badly," not "eat superfoods."** The robust, replicated, mechanistically-plausible signals are the *harms* (ultra-processed food, SSBs, excess processed meat) and *adiposity*; the "beneficial food" signals are real but observational, confounded, and small once you account for healthy-user effects.

- **The highest-value moves** are subtractive and weight-related: avoid ultra-processed food and sugar-sweetened beverages (the one food with a causal intake/weight RCT, #4), keep processed meat low (#5), and **keep BMI in the ~20–25 range / waist in check** (#11, #13 — the causally-cleanest signal, though intentional weight loss isn't proven on hard endpoints, #15).
- **Pattern > individual foods:** a Mediterranean / high-fiber / plant-*forward* (not necessarily vegetarian) pattern captures most of the benefit; DASH is the one pattern with a clean causal (BP) win (#7). Plant-based done badly (refined grains, sugar) is *worse*, not better (#2).
- **Fiber:** aim for a high-fiber diet (most-vs-least ≈ 15–30% lower mortality association), but there's no proven magic threshold — the "25–29 g plateau" was refuted (#19). More plants, less refining.
- **Skip the hype:** omega-3 *supplements* don't reproduce fish-the-food and raise AF risk (#9); coffee is fine and probably net-positive but not a proven lever (#10); fasting/CR/TRE buy nothing beyond the calorie reduction they cause (#16); protein-restriction-for-longevity is rodent-grade — favour *adequate* protein for muscle (#14).
- **Protein dose:** ~1.0–1.2 g/kg for an older adult (muscle preservation) is the better-supported target than restriction; plant protein is the cleaner association but largely a food-source/healthy-user effect.

## Actions (behavioral, checkable)
- [ ] **Minimize ultra-processed food and sugar-sweetened beverages** — the most defensible dietary harms (UPF has the one causal weight-gain RCT; SSBs have the clearest mechanism).
- [ ] **Keep BMI ~20–25 and waist in check** — adiposity is the causally-cleanest nutrition signal once reverse causation is stripped out. (Caveat: *intentional* weight loss isn't proven to cut hard CV events, so frame this as "don't gain," not "weight-loss is a guaranteed fix.")
- [ ] **Eat a plant-forward, high-fiber, Mediterranean-style pattern** — captures most of the benefit; you don't need to be vegetarian (and badly-done plant-based is worse).
- [ ] **Keep processed meat low**; unprocessed red meat is a weaker/low-certainty signal — moderate, don't agonize.
- [ ] **Get adequate protein (~1.0–1.2 g/kg), favouring plant + lean sources** — for muscle preservation; ignore protein-restriction-for-longevity claims.
- [ ] **Don't chase supplements or fasting protocols** — fish oil doesn't replace fish (and raises AF risk); fasting/CR/TRE add nothing beyond calorie control. Coffee (≤3–4 cups) is fine.
- [ ] If hypertensive, **adopt DASH** — the one diet with proven causal BP lowering (~−7/−4 mmHg).

## Caveats / population modifiers
- **Healthy-user confounding is the dominant, pervasive threat** — flagged for every observational row. Diet-quality adherers and plant-eaters are systematically leaner, more active, non-smoking, higher-SES; authors of every cohort acknowledge residual confounding. The big associations are real but weak *levers*.
- **Three different failure modes, same lesson:** (a) patterns/foods → *self-selection confounding* (effect shrinks observational→RCT); (b) omega-3 → *food-vs-supplement gap* (reductionist nutrients don't reproduce whole-food signals); (c) fasting/CR → *rodent over-extrapolation* (no human hard-endpoint data). All argue against superfood/supplement/protocol optimization.
- **Adiposity is the exception that's causally cleaner** — large pooled cohorts with reverse-causation handled by exclusion (never-smokers, drop early deaths) give a robust J-curve. BUT the one intentional-weight-loss RCT on hard endpoints (Look AHEAD) was null, so "lose weight → live longer" is association, not proven causation. Best read: *avoid gaining* excess adiposity; weight-loss benefit on hard endpoints is genuinely uncertain.
- **Reversibility is SLOW, not irreversible** — cardiometabolic markers, weight, BP, lipids recover over weeks–months to years; no "banking," but also no permanent damage from a period of poor diet (except where it already triggered atherosclerosis/cancer, which live in those systems). DASH-BP is the fastest-responding node.
- **Absolute vs relative risk:** several scary relative risks (processed meat +18% colorectal) are small in *absolute* terms; the GRADE certainty for single-food claims is genuinely low (#18). Don't over-weight a single food.
- **Population fit:** the protein/sarcopenia guidance is most relevant for *older* adults; the BMI nadir and curves are from large multi-continent cohorts (mostly mitigated for smoking/illness confounding).

## Open questions
*(also copied to `synthesis/open-questions.md`)*
- **PREDIMED** (the Mediterranean-diet RCT, primary MACE HR ~0.69–0.72 in high-risk adults) was crowded out of verification across runs and is **not independently confirmed here** — the best-known diet RCT remains a gap; its well-known result is consistent with #1/#7 but treat as not-yet-verified-in-project-tiers. (DASH-BP #7 already anchors the causal-diet story.)
- **Sodium → hard endpoints / the PURE J-curve** — DASH/sodium lower BP (surrogate), but whether sodium *reduction* cuts hard CVD events, and the contested very-low-sodium-harm (PURE) signal, were not verified. Added-sugar (beyond SSBs) specifics also un-pinned.
- **Does the Look AHEAD null generalize beyond diabetics?** No RCT of intentional weight loss powered for hard endpoints exists in a non-diabetic population — so the causal benefit of weight loss on mortality is genuinely untested outside diabetes.
- **Any human causal evidence for protein-restriction/mTOR longevity?** Currently entirely rodent/mechanistic (T3). And the muscle-preservation side (~1.0–1.2 g/kg) was asserted as stronger but its specific RCT magnitudes weren't in the confirmed set.
- **Fiber dose-response shape** — the clean "25–29 g plateau" was refuted; is benefit monotonic-with-attenuation or is there a true ceiling?
