## Scope
Teeth/enamel/gums, hearing, vision. **The "irreversible hardware" lever** — cochlear hair cells, retinal/optic neurons, tooth enamel, and alveolar bone don't regenerate, so prevention & timely correction dominate. Hearing loss is the single largest *modifiable* dementia risk factor.

- In: age-related HEARING loss & hearing aids/amplification (and the dementia/falls/depression links); VISION — cataract, age-related macular degeneration, glaucoma, uncorrected refractive error (and the dementia/falls/mortality links); ORAL health — periodontal disease, tooth loss/edentulism (and the systemic CVD/mortality/dementia associations); the IRREVERSIBILITY framing and where prevention (noise protection, correction, dental hygiene) is the only lever.
- Out (and where it lives instead): oral-CANCER screening & tobacco→oral-cancer mortality → `substances` / `medical-screening`; dental erosion driven by sugar/SSBs → mechanism noted here but `nutrition-metabolic` owns sugar; diabetic retinopathy management → `medical-screening`/`metabolic`; smell/taste loss (minor — note only); cataract *surgery* as a procedure → the dementia/falls *outcome* stays here, surgical logistics out.

## Dose / threshold
**The 80/20: prevent the permanent damage, and correct the *treatable* sensory losses early — especially if you're already at cognitive risk.** This lever has little "dose" and a lot of "fix it before it compounds."

- **Hearing:** protect against noise (the damage is irreversible), and **get hearing aids early if you have loss** — the ACHIEVE RCT shows real cognitive benefit *in higher-risk older adults* (the people most likely to be reading this later in life), even though it didn't help healthy volunteers. Don't wait years to address hearing loss.
- **Vision:** correct refractive error (glasses) and **get cataracts removed** when they impair vision — the near-causal evidence (vision-restoration → lower dementia, glaucoma-surgery negative control) is the best in this lever, even if not RCT-proven. AMD/glaucoma/DR are irreversible, so the lever there is prevention/early detection (overlaps medical-screening).
- **Oral:** keep your teeth via basic hygiene and regular dental care — but for the **right reason**: *chewing function and nutrition*, not because gum disease causes heart attacks or dementia (MR says it doesn't). If you do lose teeth, **use dentures** — restored function appears to matter.
- **What NOT to believe:** "treat your gums to prevent heart disease/Alzheimer's." The treatment RCTs move only surrogates; MR is null; the P. gingivalis trial failed.

## Actions (behavioral, checkable)
- [ ] **Protect your hearing from loud noise** (the damage is permanent) — earplugs at concerts/power tools, volume limits.
- [ ] **Get hearing tested and use hearing aids early if you have loss** — strongest cognitive benefit in those already at risk (ACHIEVE). Don't normalize years of untreated loss.
- [ ] **Correct vision: wear your glasses, and get cataract surgery when cataracts impair you** — the best near-causal cognition lever here; also reduces falls.
- [ ] **Keep functional teeth** (brush/floss, regular dental care) for chewing & nutrition; if you lose teeth, **get and wear dentures**.
- [ ] **Don't** buy the "gum disease causes heart disease/dementia, so periodontal treatment will prevent them" pitch — keep your gums healthy for your *mouth*, and rely on the actual CV/dementia levers elsewhere.
- [ ] Treat sensory correction as **cognitive-reserve maintenance**, especially from midlife on — these are among the few *modifiable* dementia levers.

## Caveats / population modifiers
- **Irreversibility is the defining axis.** Cochlear hair cells, retinal/optic neurons, and tooth enamel/alveolar bone don't regenerate — so unlike most levers, "recovery" isn't available; the value is in *prevention* and *compensation* (aids, surgery, dentures, glasses). This is why early action matters more here than dose.
- **Causal evidence is thin and the probes narrow it.** The associations are large but mostly observational with heavy confounding/reverse causation. The three causal probes all *deflate*: ACHIEVE is null overall (benefit only in high-risk), cataract→dementia is contested with no RCT, and periodontitis→systemic is **MR-null**. Trust the probes over the raw HRs.
- **Reverse causation is everywhere:** early dementia reduces auditory/visual engagement and oral self-care, so part of every sensory→dementia association runs backward. Washout analyses (hearing) and negative controls (cataract) partially address it but don't eliminate it.
- **Benefit concentrates in the already-at-risk** (ACHIEVE's lesson) and in *midlife-onward* — these are dementia-prevention levers most relevant as you age, less so for a healthy young adult.
- **Oral health's defensible rationale is function/nutrition** (chewing, malnutrition, QoL), which is real and independent of the debunked systemic-disease story — but even that evidence is mostly cross-sectional.
- **Magnitudes are inflated in popular framing:** the "hearing loss is 8% of dementia" / "vision 19%" figures are upper bounds; newer data put them lower. The direction is solid; don't over-rank on the headline PAF.

## Open questions
*(also copied to `synthesis/open-questions.md`)*
- **No RCT of cataract surgery or refractive correction on dementia/falls/mortality** exists — the vision lever rests entirely on observational + negative-control inference. Would an RCT replicate the ~25% signal, and why do the 2024 meta-analyses conflict (RR 0.74 vs 0.85 NS)?
- **Falls** — vision (and hearing) impairment plausibly raise falls (a mortality pathway) but no hazard ratio survived verification; the falls-prevention *intervention* evidence lives in the exercise lever (balance training).
- **Which PAF is right for ranking** — hearing 8% vs ~5% (newer); vision 19% (cross-sectional) vs 2% (Lancet)? The magnitude materially affects how high these rank.
- **Does treating hearing loss reduce dementia *incidence* (not just slow cognitive-test decline)** over longer follow-up, and beyond the high-risk subgroup? ACHIEVE was 3 years on a cognitive-test surrogate.
- **Periodontal treatment hard-endpoint RCT** — none exists; the surrogate effects (BP, HbA1c) are real but attenuate. Would a large/long trial show any hard-endpoint benefit, or is the MR-null the final word?
