# Lever: Stress & Social

> Status: RESEARCHED — session 5 (2026-06-22/23). All 3 sub-levers done: **SOCIAL CONNECTION** (rows 1–16, S143–S157), **CHRONIC STRESS** (rows 17–24, S158–S166), **PURPOSE/MEANING/WELL-BEING** (rows 25–32, S167–S175). 5 deep-research runs (3 resumed through usage limits).
> Headline: this is the project's clearest **"observational association ≫ causal footprint"** lever. (1) Loneliness/isolation track ~26–50% mortality differences (rivalling smoking/obesity *as observational magnitudes*), but every intervention RCT moves only self-reported loneliness — none ever measured a hard endpoint — and MR shows loneliness→CVD trivial (OR ~1.0) / loneliness→Alzheimer's null. (2) Chronic/work stress is genuinely *modest* (job strain→CHD HR 1.23, PAR 3.4%, pub-bias-inflated); the "caregiving kills" headline didn't replicate (caregivers live *longer*); subjective stress doesn't map onto mortality; **no RCT shows stress-reduction moves any hard endpoint** (AHA/Cochrane). (3) **Purpose/optimism is the strongest sub-lever** — depression-robust dementia signal (HR ~0.76) + the project's one supportive MR (well-being→healthspan/lifespan) — but still no RCT, and the effect attenuates with adjustment. Across all three, the popular "loneliness/stress/lack-of-purpose is as deadly as smoking / adds years" framing outruns the causal evidence; the defensible read is "good for well-being, plausibly protective, largely causally unproven."

## Scope
Chronic stress, loneliness, social connection, sense of purpose. Mortality effect sizes rival physical risk factors *in observational data* — the central question is how much of that is causal and modifiable.

- In: **social connection / social isolation / loneliness / living-alone** (distinct constructs) and their links to all-cause mortality, CVD/stroke, dementia/cognition; loneliness-reduction & social interventions (befriending, social prescribing, CBT-for-loneliness, volunteering); **chronic psychological stress** (job strain, caregiver stress, allostatic load) → mortality/CVD; **sense of purpose / meaning / psychological well-being / optimism** → mortality/dementia. The causal/interventional question is the whole point of this lever.
- Out (and where it lives instead): clinical **depression/anxiety as diseases** and their drug/psychotherapy treatment — noted here as a dominant confounder/mediator but not the focus; **substance use as stress-coping / social drinking** → `substances`; **exercise as a stress/mood intervention** → `exercise` (mood benefit noted there); **sleep–stress bidirectionality** → `sleep`; bereavement-as-acute-event medical sequelae → noted only.

## Dose / threshold

### Social connection
**The 80/20 here is counterintuitive: the association is one of the strongest in the project, but it is the *least causally validated* lever so far — so treat social connection as a high-value-but-unproven target, and don't expect a "dose" to buy measurable longevity.**

- **Quality > quantity > living arrangement.** The effect lives in rich, multidimensional integration (OR 1.91), not in the crude fact of living with someone (OR 1.19, NS). Aim for *engaged* relationships, not just co-residence or headcount.
- **If you intervene, target social *cognition*, not just contact.** The only intervention class that reliably moves loneliness is CBT-style work on maladaptive social thinking; "just add a befriending program / social prescription" is ~null on the surrogate and untested on anything that matters.
- **Don't over-promise the payoff.** No RCT has shown that fixing loneliness lowers mortality/CVD/dementia, and MR says the causal CVD effect is tiny and the Alzheimer's effect is zero. The honest framing: social connection is plausibly protective, clearly good for well-being, and *worth maintaining* — but the hard-endpoint causal case is unproven.
- **Persistence is the worst state.** The highest observational risk is in *chronically* isolated/lonely people; avoiding sustained isolation is the most defensible target even if "recovery" isn't proven.

### Chronic stress
**The 80/20: chronic stress is a *small* lever dressed up as a big one. The hard-endpoint effects are modest, mostly route through standard cardiometabolic risk factors, and no stress-reduction intervention has been shown to extend life — so manage stress for quality of life, and get the longevity payoff from the standard risk factors it travels with.**

- **Don't over-weight "stress" as an independent killer.** Job strain's causal share of CHD is ~3.4% (vs ~36% for smoking) and the literature overstates even that. The biggest stress-adjacent wins are the *downstream* targets that already have their own levers: blood pressure, lipids, glucose, smoking, sleep, activity.
- **Feeling stressed is not the same as being at risk.** Subjective stress and mortality dissociate (caregivers feel worse, live longer). Use stress-management for *how you feel and function*, not as a presumed mortality intervention.
- **Stress-reduction (MBSR/meditation/CBT) is worth doing for well-being, BP, and anxiety — but treat the hard-endpoint benefit as unproven.** No RCT shows it cuts CVD events/mortality; it does modestly move surrogates (perceived stress, BP, cortisol). It overlaps the `sleep` (CBT-I) and `exercise` (mood) levers.
- **If stress is harming sleep, diet, drinking, or activity, fix *those* (they're the high-certainty levers).** The most defensible "stress" action is preventing it from degrading the behaviors that demonstrably matter.

### Purpose & well-being
**The 80/20: of the three sub-levers, purpose/optimism has the best evidence — robust, depression-independent associations (especially with dementia) plus one supportive MR — so it's the one most worth treating as a real, if unproven, longevity-adjacent target, not just a quality-of-life nicety.**

- **Cultivate a sense of purpose** (goals, engagement, something that gives life meaning) — the dementia association (HR ~0.76) survives depression adjustment and is consistent across large cohorts, and it's the construct with the only causal probe pointing the right way.
- **Optimism counts, but don't over-read it** — the longevity signal is real but ~40% runs through health behaviors and can vanish under full adjustment. Treat "stay engaged and forward-looking" as supportive of the behaviors that matter, not a standalone life-extender.
- **Don't buy the "ikigai adds years" causal pitch.** The big single-cohort HRs are minimally adjusted; the honest pooled effect is ~17% (RR 0.83), no RCT proves causation, and Blue Zones data quality is contested. Pursue purpose because it's robustly *associated* with better aging and is good in itself — not because a trial showed it adds years.

## Actions (behavioral, checkable)
*(Sub-levers 1–3.)*

### Social connection
- [ ] **Maintain a few high-quality, active relationships** (the "complex integration" that carries the real signal) rather than optimizing for living arrangement or contact count.
- [ ] **Avoid sliding into *persistent* isolation/loneliness** — the chronic state carries the steepest risk; treat re-connection as worth doing even though risk-reversal isn't proven.
- [ ] If loneliness is a problem, **prefer approaches that address how you think about social situations (CBT-style)** over passive "more contact" programs — that's the only class with RCT support (on self-report).
- [ ] **Calibrate expectations:** value connection for well-being and probable (not proven) health benefit; don't treat "cure loneliness → live longer" as established, and discount the "as deadly as smoking" framing.

### Chronic stress
- [ ] **Don't treat "stress" as a top-tier longevity lever** — its independent causal footprint is small; put that effort into BP/lipids/glucose/smoking/sleep/activity (their own levers), which is where stress's damage actually routes.
- [ ] **Use stress-reduction (MBSR / CBT / meditation / exercise) for well-being, BP, and anxiety** — real surrogate benefits — without expecting a proven mortality payoff.
- [ ] **Protect the high-certainty behaviors from stress spillover** (sleep, diet, drinking, activity) — that's the most defensible stress-related action.
- [ ] **Don't fear caregiving as a death sentence** — the "caregiver strain kills" claim didn't replicate; caregivers as a group live as long or longer. Address caregiver *distress* for its own sake.

### Purpose & well-being
- [ ] **Cultivate and protect a sense of purpose** (goals, meaningful activity, engagement) — the best-evidenced construct in this lever, with a depression-robust dementia association.
- [ ] **Stay engaged/forward-looking**, but treat optimism's longevity benefit as partly behavior-mediated — channel it into the high-certainty behaviors (activity, sleep, not smoking).
- [ ] **Pursue purpose for its own sake and its robust associations — not because a trial proved it adds years** (it hasn't). Discount "ikigai/Blue Zones adds X years" causal claims.

## Caveats / population modifiers

### Social connection
- **This is the project's clearest association ≠ causation lever.** Observational signal is enormous and consistent (millions of participants); causal/interventional support is nearly absent (surrogate-only RCTs, trivial/null MR). Trust the probes over the raw HRs.
- **Depression is the load-bearing confounder/mediator** and is handled inconsistently across studies — it drives the conflicting loneliness→dementia results (#7 vs #8). Whether depression is a confounder, mediator, or collider determines whether loneliness is an "independent" risk factor.
- **Reverse causation is everywhere** (illness/frailty/prodromal dementia → isolation); partially mitigated, never excluded, especially for dementia's long prodrome.
- **UK Biobank dominance + heterogeneity:** much of the dementia/reversibility data is one healthier-than-average volunteer cohort; meta-analytic I² is frequently 85–95%.
- **Constructs are not interchangeable:** objective isolation generally has the more robust hard-endpoint associations; subjective loneliness is weaker and dataset-dependent; "living alone" is the weakest proxy.

### Chronic stress
- **Effect sizes are modest and inflated by the literature.** Job strain→CHD is HR ~1.23 (likely ~1.16 once publication bias is accounted for), PAR 3.4%. This is a *small* lever; the "stress kills" narrative substantially outruns the data.
- **Comparator choice flips the caregiver result** — high-strain caregivers show no excess vs noncaregivers but do vs no-strain caregivers within-cohort. The caregiver-survival advantage is widely attributed to the **"healthy caregiver effect"** (selection: healthier people can provide care), so don't read it as "caregiving protects" either.
- **Allostatic load is partly circular:** the AL score is largely conventional cardiometabolic biomarkers, so "AL predicts CVD" is mostly the standard risk factors talking — it does NOT isolate a stress-specific physiological pathway, and doesn't show reducing AL reduces events.
- **The causal cupboard is bare:** no MR or quasi-experimental evidence on chronic stress→hard endpoints surfaced; the only T1 *interventional* evidence is a **negative** finding (no RCT moves hard endpoints).
- **Acute vs chronic:** this lever is about *chronic* stress; acute-stress events (bereavement, Takotsubo, MI triggers) are real but out of scope here and not the longevity question.

### Purpose & well-being
- **Strongest sub-lever, but still observational + one MR.** The associations survive depression adjustment (genuinely "not just absence of depression") and the dementia signal is consistent — but there is still **no RCT** on hard endpoints, so causation rests on cohorts + a single, caveated MR (Ye 2024).
- **Reverse causation & over-/under-adjustment cut both ways.** Prodromal illness (esp. preclinical dementia, ~5-yr windows) lowers reported purpose, inflating associations; meanwhile health behaviors are confounders *and* mediators, so "fully adjusted" HRs may over-correct toward null. Both raw and adjusted estimates can mislead.
- **Magnitude is adjustment-dependent and over-hyped:** popular "HR ~2.4" / "ikigai adds years" figures are minimally adjusted single-cohort results; the robust pooled mortality effect is ~17% (RR 0.83).
- **Generalizability:** much optimism evidence is women-only / high-SES Western cohorts (NHS, WHI); ASPREE suggests the optimism–mortality effect may concentrate in the oldest-old and can disappear after full adjustment.
- **Overlaps depression and the other sub-levers.** Purpose, optimism, low loneliness, and low depression travel together; their "independent" effects are only as clean as the (usually single, brief) depression screen used to separate them.

## Open questions
*(also copied to `synthesis/open-questions.md`)*
- **Has any RCT/quasi-experiment powered for a HARD endpoint** (mortality, incident CVD, incident dementia) of a social/loneliness intervention ever been run or registered? The entire causal case hinges on this gap — currently empty.
- **Does transitioning OUT of loneliness/isolation recover excess risk, and over what timeframe?** Only persistent-vs-never contrasts exist; no transition-with-hard-endpoint analysis.
- **Why do two UK Biobank analyses disagree** on whether loneliness→dementia survives depression adjustment — i.e., is depression a confounder, mediator, or collider here?
- **Is the loneliness↔depression relationship causal and in which direction** (bidirectional MR was requested but not resolved)?
- **(Stress) Is there ANY MR / quasi-experimental evidence that chronic stress causally drives CVD/mortality?** None surfaced — the central causal-inference gap.
- **(Stress) Do stress-reduction interventions move hard endpoints in *secondary* prevention** (recurrent cardiac events, dementia incidence), or is the surrogate-only verdict universal? Verified evidence only covered primary-prevention CVD mortality.
- **(Stress) INTERHEART psychosocial-stress PAR for MI** (~30%, the famous high figure) did not survive verification this run — re-derive if stress sits near a ranking boundary, but note it's case-control (weaker than the prospective HR 1.23).
- **(Stress) Does cortisol/HPA-axis dysregulation *specifically* predict hard endpoints** independent of conventional cardiometabolic risk, or is allostatic load just repackaging standard risk factors?
- **(Purpose) Does ANY RCT of a purpose-/positive-psychology intervention move a HARD endpoint**, or have all such trials reported only surrogates? (Same gap as the other two sub-levers — needs a trial-registry search to confirm.)
- **(Purpose) Does the dementia association survive longer follow-up / landmark analyses** that exclude prodromal cases beyond the ~5-yr UK Biobank window, and adjustment for baseline cognition?
- **(Purpose) Can the Ye 2024 MR be replicated** with cleaner well-being instruments separated from depression genetics, with MR-Egger/weighted-median confirming directionality (the disputed methodology)?
- **(Purpose) Is the optimism benefit concentrated in the oldest-old** (ASPREE 77+) vs a general adult effect, and does it generalize beyond female/high-SES Western cohorts?
